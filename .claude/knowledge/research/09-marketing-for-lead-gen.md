## The website's actual marketing job

Buyers **don't discover Tipco on the website** — they find it on Google / IndiaMART / referrals / trade shows, then visit to decide *"can I trust these people with a ₹5-lakh machine?"* So the site is a **mid/late-funnel trust-and-spec machine**, not a billboard. Every design choice follows: **fast, spec-rich, trust-heavy, easy to contact** — not flashy. Success metric = **qualified quote requests, not visits.**

---

## The 6 things that turn a visitor into a lead

1. **Effortless contact** — WhatsApp (dominant in Indian B2B) + call + short quote form. Never form-wall. Form should carry product context ("Enquiry: Dyno Mill THM-25").
2. **Clear CTA on every page, above the fold** — worded **"Get Price" / "Request Quote"**, not "Submit."
3. **A lead magnet** — downloadable **spec-sheet / catalogue PDF** for a name + phone. Captures the *champion* who forwards it to boss/finance. **Zero datasheets today = biggest lead-capture gap.**
4. **Trust signals everywhere** — BSE-listed, 35+ yrs, ISO, 2 factories, named clients. Answers the #1 fear ("will I get blamed?"). Add a homepage **stats band**.
5. **Technical depth + TCO story** — specs = 40% of the decision, TCO = 30%. Complete spec tables + reframe from price to lifecycle cost (low maintenance, genuine spares, uptime, energy).
6. **Kill the top fears on-page** — downtime, spare-parts hostage, "will it hit fineness," "will you exist in 10 yrs." Each needs a specific answer (reliability proof, spares assurance, trial-run offer, public-company credibility). Full map in [05 §7](05-buyer-personas-deep-dive.md).

## What's still MISSING from a marketing POV (leads don't come from the site alone)

| Gap | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **No clear positioning / USP line** | Need one sticky claim — e.g. *"Global-quality process machinery at Indian pricing, from a listed company."* Should headline the whole site. Nothing crisp exists today. |
| **Lead response speed** ⚠ biggest lead killer | B2B buyers pick whoever **replies fastest**; IndiaMART ranks by response rate. A web lead ignored a day is dead. Need a process/CRM to respond in **minutes**. |
| **Paid ads while SEO matures** | SEO takes months; **Google Ads** on high-intent terms ("dyno mill price") buys leads today. Tipco already runs Google Ads — point them at the bead-mill page + track conversions. |
| **Email / nurture** | Sales cycle = weeks–months, multi-visit. Capture email, send catalogue, follow up. One visit rarely closes capital equipment. |
| **Reviews / social proof** | Google Business reviews + IndiaMART ratings are trust currency competitors use. Collect actively. |
| **Lead tracking (which page/channel converts)** | Without conversion tracking (SEO backlog [07](07-seo-implementation-backlog.md)), can't tell which spend works. Measure **lead quality, not traffic.** |

## The mindset shift

Marketing for a ₹5-lakh machine isn't "get clicks" — it's **"reduce the buyer's fear and make him look smart to his boss."** Every credibility signal, spec sheet, and fast reply does that job. These 6 mechanics matter most on the **Bead Mill / Dyno Mill page** (see [06](06-lead-gen-seo-priority.md)) — where the ready-to-buy traffic lands.

## Highest-leverage next step

Draft the **positioning / USP + homepage message** — the one foundational piece everything else hangs on. Then build the consolidated Bead Mill page with all 6 mechanics wired in.
