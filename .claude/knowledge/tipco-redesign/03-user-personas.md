## Persona 1: Procurement Manager / Purchase Head

**Name (placeholder):** Rajesh Verma
**Age:** 38–52
**Role:** Purchase Manager / VP Operations at a mid-to-large paint or pharma manufacturer
**Location:** Mumbai, Pune, Delhi, Ahmedabad, Chennai (major industrial hubs)

### Goals
- Source reliable industrial machinery at competitive prices
- Reduce vendor risk — needs to justify purchase to management
- Get multiple quotes quickly to compare
- Verify supplier credibility before engaging

### Behavior on the website
- Arrives via Google search ("bead mill manufacturer India", "dyno mill price")
- Skips hero and goes straight to products
- Looks for: product specs, company credentials (certificates, client list), contact details
- Will not fill a long form — wants to call or WhatsApp directly
- May visit 3–5 competitor sites in the same session

### Frustrations with current Tipco site
- 7.2 second load time — likely bounces before page loads
- Can't find spec sheets or PDF datasheets per product
- Can't easily compare models within a category
- Trust signals (certifications, clients) are buried at the bottom

### What this persona needs from the redesign
- Product specs visible without clicking through 3 levels
- Downloadable PDF datasheet per product
- Client logos and certifications above the fold or near products
- 1-click enquiry (WhatsApp or short form) on every product page
- Company credibility signals early in the page

## Persona 2: R&D / Lab Technician

**Name (placeholder):** Priya Sharma
**Age:** 26–40
**Role:** R&D Chemist, Lab Manager, or Process Engineer at pharma/cosmetics/food company
**Location:** Any major industrial city; often browses at their office desk

### Goals
- Find a lab-scale machine for pilot testing
- Understand technical specifications in detail (RPM, capacity, material of construction)
- Determine if machine is suitable for their specific formulation (viscosity, particle size)
- May need to convince their manager to approve the purchase

### Behavior on the website
- Arrives via specific product search ("lab high speed disperser 2L", "bead mill pharma")
- Reads product pages thoroughly — cares about specs, materials, MOC
- May visit multiple times before enquiring
- Likely to download a catalogue or request a brochure
- Will use WhatsApp for quick technical questions

### Frustrations with current Tipco site
- Technical spec tables exist on product pages (good!) but are hard to find
- No way to compare two lab models side by side
- Image gallery doesn't show machine dimensions or detail shots
- No FAQ or application notes per machine type

### What this persona needs from the redesign
- Detailed specs upfront on product pages (not hidden in tabs)
- Application-specific content: "Suitable for: pharma, cosmetics, food" with explanation
- High-quality machine images showing size reference and detail
- Easy access to catalogue PDF
- WhatsApp link for quick technical questions

## Secondary Audience: Website Walk-in / Exhibition Follow-up

These are visitors who have already met Tipco at a trade show (Printpack, CPhI, etc.) and are visiting the site to verify credibility or find a product they were shown.

**Behavior:** Direct URL type-in or branded search. They already know Tipco.
**Need:** Confirmation that the company is credible. They will look at client list, certificates, and event photos.
**Design implication:** Client logos, certifications, and an "Events" section serve this audience.

---

## What This Means for Page Priority

| Persona | Most important pages |
|---------|---------------------|
| Procurement Manager | Homepage → Category page → Product detail → Enquiry |
| Lab Technician | Product detail → Specs → Catalogue → WhatsApp |
| Exhibition follow-up | Homepage → About → Clients → Contact |

The product detail page serves all three personas — it is the single highest-priority page to design well.
